Why mh11wi Anyways?

May 4, 2023

You may have been wondering why my username is "mh11wi". And while I wish had a better answer for you, it's just something I chose a long time ago that made sense at the time. Kind of like legacy code that's still chugging along! Over the years, though, the name has really grown on me. It has become my 'brand' in a way — I use it for email, various profiles, and of course this website. I've even begun reading mh11wi as a whole word (mill-wee) rather than each character individually in my head. That said, it's probably best to share the meaning behind these characters, before that context is lost.

A Little History

I originally created this account to contribute to the Caribou Contests private repository when I was working for my professor at Brock University (you can read more on how this came about in my first blog post). Like the other students working for Caribou at the time, I used my Brock login — mh11wi — to sign into Caribou. And so for consistency, I decided to use the same username on GitHub.

Did I create my own Brock login? No. It was automatically generated for me when I first became a student. The naming convention seems to be as follows:

  • mh — my first and last initials,
  • 11 — the year the account was created (2011),
  • wi — two random letters to guarantee uniqueness in their system.

That's really all there is to it. No deeper meaning; but we are definitely going to ignore the fact that 2011 was over a decade ago now.

So Why Not Change It?

After finishing school and my time with Caribou, I wanted to create a professional presence online. Namely, a portfolio to share my work and other experiences. And that is how I learned about GitHub Pages! I considered changing my username at this time, knowing mh11wi would become the subdomain of my website. But I honestly couldn't think of anything better — especially since what I wanted was a username I could use across different accounts, profiles, and email addresses that A) wouldn't already be taken, and B) still representative of myself.

Surprisingly, "Madeleine Hill" is more common of name than you'd think. No really, Google it! On many platforms, usernames like "mhill" and "hillm", or even "MadeleineHill" and "madeleine_hill" already exist. And I didn't want to just tack on a bunch of numbers to the end.

On the other hand, it's probably for the best, because frankly, my name gets misspelled a lot. More often than not I'm called "Madeline" by mistake. And me, being a little socially awkward, usually just accepts it. Lately I've been trying to avoid the issue by joking that my name is like the cake, not the cartoon. Which works well in person, but an online platform is not going to correct you in the same way if you type the wrong name.

It's funny, because all of this is just reminding me of the famous quote:

There are only two hard things in Computer Science: cache invalidation and naming things.

— Phil Karlton

While mh11wi may not traditionally seem like a good name, it's short, it's unique, and has served me well over the last few years.

Lessons Learned

  • It's true, naming things really is hard.
  • If somebody misspells or mispronounces your name, it's better to correct them.
  • For better searchability, try to be as consistent as possible with naming.
